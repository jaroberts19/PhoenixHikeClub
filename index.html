Phoenix Hike Club

About Us

Who are we? We're a group of people local to the city of Phoenix, Arizona and want to apprecite the natural beauties that surround it. We organize hikes and related activities that further the enjoyment, appreciation, and preservation of both the hikers and the land.

Upcoming Events

Apache Wash Trailhead

9am on October 10, 2020

This preserve has a well developed system of trails in two areas. We’ll hike the area from the Apache Wash Trailhead, off E Sonoran Desert Drive. This hike is roughly 7.8 miles. Starting at the Apache Wash Trailhead, we’ll take Sidewinder trail, heading for the Apache Vista. Go up the Vista (of course), then retrace our steps, down to Sidewinder where it intersects with the Apache Wash Loop. We’ll then take the Apache Wash Loop all the way around back to the Trailhead. The trails are very well marked. We should see some hot air balloons.

Pipeline Canyon

10am on October 17, 2020

Starting from the Pipeline Canyon Day Use Area and Trailhead (#4 on the park map) we’ll cross the floating bridge (not sure if it will be floating due to low water levels). About 1.4 miles along the trail we’ll take a spur to the newly built Yavapai Point trail and climb 0.9 miles to a gorgeous vantage point. From up here we’ll have a truly beautiful view of Lake Pleasant and surrounding mountains. It is a bit of a climb, but worth the effort. Then we’ll come back down and continue on the Yavapai Point trail to the Cottonwood Day use and Trailhead. At this point we can decide if we want to extend the hike to 8.1 miles by taking the Cottonwood Trail (this will add 2.4 miles), or just have a snack and return.

Elephant Mountain

8am on October 24, 2020

This hike will cover most of the conservation area with the main attraction being Elephant Mountain. The Elephant Mountain Trail will be exploratory for me. We will start on the Spur Cross trail to the Tortuga trail up to Elephant Mountain Trail. The trail will take us in and out of some washes and up some steep climbs before we reach some ruins. The views are said to be splendid. After descending Elephant Mountain we will continue on the Spur Cross trail to the edge of the conservation area and then turn back to complete the loop around the park via the Spur Cross trail and the Dragonfly trail through the Jewel of the Creek Preserve.
